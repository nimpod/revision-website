Introduction to the Physical Layer

Layer 1 - The Physical Layer deals with bit-level transmission between different devices.

However, that binary data is kind of an abstraction from what they are in reality.

If you cut open a fibre optic cable you wouldn't see 1's and 0's pouring out of the wire.

There are signals travelling through the wire.

Those signals have voltage levels.

Then what we'll do is choose a reliability margin:

  • a number that says everything above it = 1, and everything below it = 0
  • a reliability margin of 5V is common
    • so 1V=0; 2V=0; 3V=0; 4V=0; 5V=0; 6V=1; 7V=1; 8V=1, etc...
